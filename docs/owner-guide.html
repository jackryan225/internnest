A match, step by step

What happens in the ~8 seconds after a student clicks "Find My Internship Matches". This is the algorithm.

The student tells us who they are

Major, class year, GPA, dream industry & role, location, skills, prior experience. More detail in → better matches out.

The site shortlists 15 from the 1,000+ database

Instant, rule-based filtering: right industry first, then hard eligibility. If a posting states a minimum GPA or is juniors-only, ineligible students never see it. Ultra-selective firms (Goldman, McKinsey…) are held back from low GPAs. Location, remote preference, and skill overlap rank the rest.

Claude reads the top 8 like a career counselor

The AI scores each one 0–100 and writes the why, the missing skills, and an application tip, personally, for that student. free = Haiku (fast)premium = Opus (smarter)

The student gets their results

Free accounts see their top 3. Premium ($9.99, one-time) unlocks every match plus AI-written LinkedIn outreach messages. That model gap is the reason to pay.

They track it to the offer

Saved → Applied → Interviewing → Offer on the tracker, with one-click Export to Excel. The more they use it, the more reasons to come back.

Where the $9.99 goes

Cards never touch the site. Stripe handles the sensitive part.

Buy button → Stripe's secure checkout

The student pays on Stripe's own page. Card numbers never exist anywhere you're responsible for.

The site double-checks the receipt

When they come back, the server asks Stripe directly "did they really pay?", so unlocks can't be faked.

Premium sticks to their account

The premium flag is saved to their Supabase account, so it follows them to any device they sign in on.

Money lands in Stripe → Dillon's bank

Stripe keeps ~2.9% + 30¢ and auto-pays the rest out. ~$9.40 net per premium unlock. Nobody else touches it.

The easy way: open the project in Claude Code and say "run the scraper loop and push." It runs all of this, checks the results, and deploys. Every listing was found on a public job board, cleaned and fact-checked by AI, and its apply link tested. The machine only adds, verifies, and retires listings; it never invents them.

Making a change

No code required.

Say what you want

Open the project in Claude Code and describe it: "change the price to $14.99", "add a testimonials section", "make the logo bigger."

It edits, tests, and pushes

The change is saved to GitHub, the permanent record of every version of the site.

Vercel publishes automatically

~30 seconds after the push, the live site is updated. Anything wrong? Vercel can roll back to any earlier version with one click.
