How to Land Your First Internship (With No Experience)

If you do not have an internship yet, you are not behind — you are exactly who the system is hardest on, and exactly who a little structure helps most. Here is a plan that works without connections.

1. Get specific before you get busy

"Any internship" is the hardest thing to apply for. Pick one or two industries and a role type. Specific applications are faster to write and far more convincing.

2. Build a base resume you can tailor

One clean, one-page resume with your coursework, projects, and any work or leadership experience. You will tweak it per application — but you need a strong starting point.

3. Apply where the roles are real

Dead links and ghost postings waste your energy. Focus on verified, currently-open roles (this is the whole point of InternNest) so every hour you spend actually counts.

4. Pair every application with one outreach

A short, genuine note to someone on the team dramatically raises your odds. You do not need a connection — you need a specific, respectful message (see our outreach guide).

5. Track everything

Saved, applied, interviewing, offer. Knowing where each application stands keeps you following up at the right time instead of letting things go cold.

The takeaway: first internships go to the students who are specific, consistent, and a little brave about reaching out — not the ones who were born connected.
